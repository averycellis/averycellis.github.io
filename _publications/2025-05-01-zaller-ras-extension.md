I replicate and extend John Zaller's Receive-Accept-Sample (RAS) model of public opinion formation. I first demonstrate that the model's formal structure is compatible with both motivated reasoning and Bayesian updating frameworks — a feature rather than a flaw, given recent work showing these cognitive processes are observationally equivalent. I then extend Zaller's binary conception of political messages to define messages as continuous probability distributions, allowing opinions to be characterized by both position (mean) and clarity (variance). These extensions receive mixed but promising empirical support, with selective reception showing empirical validity consistent with contemporary research on information environments, and the mean-variance argument supported: the clarity of a message impacts not just *if* a message is accepted, but *how* it restructures belief distributions.

**Awarded Highest Honors**, Bowdoin College Department of Government & Legal Studies, May 2025.
