I replicate and extend John Zaller's Receive-Accept-Sample (RAS) model of public opinion formation. I first demonstrate that the model's formal structure is compatible with both motivated reasoning and Bayesian updating frameworks and analyze this finding in light of recent work showing the observational equivalence of these cognitive processes. I then propose an extension to Zaller's binary conception of political messages and define messages as continuous probability distributions, allowing opinions to be characterized by both position (mean) and clarity (variance). Utilizing data from the 2016 British Election Study, I find strong support for Zaller's model and mixed but promising empirical support for the proposed extensions, with selective reception and mean-variance dynamics showing the strongest validity.

**Awarded Highest Honors**, Bowdoin College Department of Government & Legal Studies, May 2025.
